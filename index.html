About Amos Tiyo

I'm a finance and economics student at Tufts University with a passion for leveraging quantitative analysis and data-driven insights to solve complex financial challenges. My journey spans three continents—from analyzing health equity funding models at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute in Boston, to driving partnership deals in East Africa, to studying sustainable development in the hill tribes of Thailand.

With hands-on experience in financial modeling, investment analysis, and data analytics, I bring a unique blend of technical expertise and global perspective to every project. Whether I'm building DCF models, conducting market research, or developing strategies for healthcare sustainability, I thrive at the intersection of finance, technology, and social impact.

Currently, I'm deepening my expertise through rigorous academic training in economics and finance, complemented by real-world experience in investment banking preparation, forex trading, and organizational development. My work has ranged from writing 55-page green energy proposals to the World Bank, to managing a personal trading portfolio, to leading cancer awareness initiatives across Kenya. I'm driven by curiosity, fueled by data, and committed to using finance as a tool for creating sustainable value—whether that's optimizing investment portfolios, improving healthcare access in low- and middle-income countries, or identifying emerging market opportunities.
